# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

The MakeitSEO Standards Programme is a technical project. We are here to build better open specifications for measuring website visibility in search engines and AI systems. We expect everyone who participates — contributors, reviewers, implementers, and observers — to behave professionally, argue with evidence, and treat disagreement as a signal to reason more carefully rather than a provocation to escalate.

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces: GitHub Issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other channel where the programme is being worked on.

---

## Standards

**We expect:**

- Technical arguments backed by reasoning and, where possible, evidence
- Disagreement with ideas expressed directly and without personal attack
- Acknowledgement when you're wrong or when new information changes your position
- Good faith assumption: assume others are trying to improve the spec, not undermine it
- Respect for the editor-led governance model during v0.1 — challenge decisions in issues, not via behaviour

**We will not tolerate:**

- Personal attacks, insults, or targeted harassment
- Discriminatory language or behaviour on any basis
- Deliberate intimidation, trolling, or sustained bad-faith participation
- Public or private harassment of project participants
- Publishing private information about another person without consent
- Any conduct that would reasonably make a participant feel unwelcome or unsafe

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces and when an individual is representing the project in public. "Representing the project" includes posting in an official capacity, using an official project communication channel, or acting as a named contributor in an external forum.

---

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behaviour may be reported to the project maintainer:

**Daniel Titton — daniel@makeitseo.io**

All reports will be reviewed promptly. The maintainer is obligated to maintain confidentiality with regard to the reporter. Reports about the maintainer may be submitted to Cloud WiFi Limited at the company address on record with Companies House (UK Co. No. 13521157).

**Enforcement actions** available to the maintainer include:

| Level | Action |
|-------|--------|
| 1 | Written warning with specific description of the violation |
| 2 | Temporary suspension from participation in project spaces |
| 3 | Permanent ban from all project spaces |

The level applied will be proportionate to the severity and frequency of the violation. The maintainer's decisions on enforcement are final during the v0.1 editor-led governance phase.
