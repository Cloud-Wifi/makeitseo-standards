## Abstract

This specification defines the measurement methodology for the GEO (Generative Engine Optimisation) pillar in the MakeitSEO Standards Programme. GEO measures inclusion as a **cited or mentioned source in AI-generated responses** across major AI engines — ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Gemini, and Google AI Overviews when functioning as a citation surface. Citation is the customer outcome GEO tracks.

GEO is one of three outcome pillars in the MakeitSEO Standards Programme, alongside SEO (traditional ranking) and AEO (deterministic answer surfaces). The three pillars compose to the overall MakeitSEO score as the arithmetic mean of their independent pillar scores. The architectural reasoning is captured in `methodology-adr/v0.1`.

GEO v0.1 is grounded in the 2026 industry evidence base on what actually predicts AI citation. The strongest finding is that **off-domain signals dominate**. Brand web mentions correlate with AI citation at r=0.664 (Ahrefs 75K-brand study). Brand search volume correlates at r=0.334 (Digital Bloom 680M-citation analysis). Backlinks correlate at r=0.218 — meaningfully weaker than brand mentions. Content freshness produces a 3.2× citation multiplier for content under 30 days old. Per-platform source preferences vary up to 615× across AI platforms.

GEO v0.1 reflects these findings in its sub-score composition. Brand web mentions, content freshness, per-platform source affinity, and structured-content density (including FAQ-structured content where applicable) are first-class sub-scores. The citation rate and mention share sub-scores remain as the principal outcome measurements but their weight has been adjusted to make room for the precursor signals that predict citation.

A runtime-compatibility sub-score routes ARC findings into GEO per `agent-runtime-compatibility/v0.1` Decision 3. This sub-score covers AI-engine runtimes (Perplexity Fetch, Gemini Fetch, OpenAI Agents SDK, Claude Agent Skills, AI Overview's citation-side runtime).

## Status

This is the v0.1 draft of GEO scoring. Of the three outcome pillar specs at v0.1, GEO has the most substantial new methodology because the 2026 evidence base has materially changed what AI citation is understood to depend on. Earlier internal framings underweighted off-domain signals (brand mentions, branded search) and treated content freshness as SEO hygiene rather than as a primary GEO signal. v0.1 corrects both.

The v0.1 → v1.0 promotion targets ~early November 2026, ~60–90 days post-launch. Weight calibration against first-cohort outcome data is expected; weights specified in v0.1 are grounded in published research correlations but not yet calibrated against MakeitSEO's own customer data.

## Definitions

### Generative engine

An AI system that produces natural-language responses to user queries, drawing on training data plus optional live retrieval, and citing sources from its retrieval set. As of May 2026 the principal generative engines are ChatGPT (OpenAI), Perplexity, Claude (Anthropic), Gemini (Google), and Google AI Overviews when functioning in citation mode.

The distinguishing property of a *generative engine* surface (as opposed to a *deterministic answer surface*) is that citation is selected probabilistically by the engine's retrieval and generation pipeline. A site cannot guarantee citation by satisfying a rule set; it can only improve the probability through signals the engine's pipeline weights.

### AI citation

A measured event where a customer's domain appears as a cited source within an AI-generated response. Citations are emitted differently by different engines: Perplexity uses inline numbered citations; ChatGPT may cite or mention without explicit link; Claude tends to mention without inline citation; Gemini varies by mode. The audit pipeline normalises citation detection across engines.

### Brand web mention

A mention of the customer's brand name (or registered variants) anywhere on the open web, with or without a clickable link. The 2026 research base identifies brand web mentions as the strongest single predictor of AI citation (r=0.664 in the Ahrefs 75K-brand study). Measurement uses DataForSEO Backlinks API for unlinked mention detection plus the standard backlink graph for linked mentions.

### Branded search volume

The volume of search queries containing the customer's brand name (or registered variants) over a defined period. The 2026 research base identifies brand search volume as the second-strongest predictor of AI citation (r=0.334 in the Digital Bloom 680M-citation analysis). Measurement uses DataForSEO Keywords Data with a branded-query filter.

### Content freshness

The age of a page's content as evidenced by `datePublished`, `dateModified`, and other temporal signals. The 2026 research base identifies content freshness as a substantial GEO predictor: pages updated within 30 days receive 3.2× more AI citations across platforms; for Perplexity specifically, content updated within 2 hours sees a 38% boost in citation probability.

### Per-platform source affinity

The platform-specific preference of each AI engine for certain source types. ChatGPT cites Wikipedia in 47.9% of top citations; Perplexity cites Reddit in 46.7%; Google AI Overview cites YouTube in 23.3%; Claude cites blogs in 43.8%. Citation patterns vary up to 615× across platforms (Superlines March 2026). A platform-agnostic off-domain measurement misrepresents the citation landscape; platform-weighted measurement captures it.

### Multi-schema and structured-content density

The breadth of schema.org types deployed on a page (Article, Product, Organization, BreadcrumbList, FAQPage, HowTo, etc.) and the density of structured content elements (statistics, quotations, structured Q&A). Pages with three or more schema types have a 13% higher likelihood of being cited by LLMs (2026 State of AI Search). Content with statistics earns 22% more AI visibility; content with quotations earns 37% more (Superlines).

### GEO sub-score

One of the eight components of the GEO pillar score at v0.1. Each sub-score measures a specific dimension of GEO performance with an explicit weight that sums to 100% across sub-scores.

### Pillar runtime sub-score

The runtime-compatibility component of the GEO sub-score set, populated by routing ARC findings from runtimes tagged with `relevant_outcome_pillar = 'geo'` (or `'multiple'` with GEO in the routing list). Specified in `agent-runtime-compatibility/v0.1`.

## Sub-scores and weights

GEO v0.1 defines eight sub-scores. Weights are evidence-grounded against the 2026 research base.

| Sub-score | Weight | What it measures |
|---|---|---|
| Citation rate | 20% | Rate of customer domain citation across AI engines for tracked queries |
| Brand web mentions | 15% | Volume and quality of unlinked brand mentions across the open web |
| Mention share | 10% | Customer's share of mentions across tracked queries vs competitors |
| Content freshness | 10% | Age and update cadence of customer's tracked pages |
| Per-platform source affinity | 10% | Customer's strength on each platform's preferred source types |
| Multi-schema + structured-content density | 10% | Schema-type breadth and statistics/quotations density on tracked pages |
| AI crawler accessibility | 10% | Whether AI engine crawlers are explicitly allowed access |
| Runtime compatibility (AI engine runtimes) | 15% | Whether AI engine runtimes can fetch and parse the site, routed from ARC |

**Total: 100%**

The weight distribution reflects four judgements:

1. **Outcome measurements still anchor the pillar.** Citation rate (20%) and mention share (10%) are the principal outcome measurements — what the customer is being scored against is, fundamentally, citation in AI responses.

2. **Off-domain precursors are first-class signals.** Brand web mentions (15%) and per-platform source affinity (10%) are not "supporting metrics" — they are the strongest published predictors of the outcome citation rate measures. The combined 25% reflects that the research base places them ahead of any single on-domain signal.

3. **Content freshness is dual-pillar but weighted differently per pillar.** In SEO it's part of content quality at modest weight; in GEO it's a primary signal at 10%. The 2026 evidence base supports the asymmetry.

4. **Runtime compatibility (15%) is higher in GEO than in AEO (10%).** AI engine runtimes are more heterogeneous in their fetch architecture than answer-surface fetchers — Perplexity, Gemini, Claude, ChatGPT, and AIO use materially different fetch patterns. Runtime invisibility for GEO purposes has more leverage than for AEO purposes.

## Sub-score methodology

### 1. Citation rate (20% of GEO pillar)

**What it measures:** the rate at which the customer's domain appears as a cited source across tracked AI engine responses.

**Method:**

1. The platform maintains a tracked-query set per customer (shared with AEO; same Wizard Step 5 input plus auto-suggestions).
2. The audit pipeline issues each tracked query to each tier-relevant AI engine via that engine's API or via DataForSEO's AI search APIs.
3. The pipeline records the full response per (query × platform) including all cited sources.
4. Citation detection normalises across engines: Perplexity's inline citations, ChatGPT's references, Claude's mentions, Gemini's source links, AIO's source cards. The implementation reference documents per-engine detection logic.
5. The sub-score is computed as:

   ```
   citation_rate_score = (
     citations_received / (tracked_queries × tier_relevant_platforms)
   ) * 100 * scaling_factor
   ```

   Where `scaling_factor` normalises against benchmark cohort data so that a typical mid-market site scores around 50, not around 5. The scaling factor is documented in the implementation reference and updated annually against cohort benchmarks.

**Why 20%:** citation rate is the most direct outcome measurement in GEO. It's reduced from earlier framings (30%) because the precursor signals (brand mentions, freshness, source affinity) are now first-class sub-scores. The combined precursor-signal weight (45%) plus the outcome weight (20%) ensures the pillar measures both the outcome and the levers that predict it.

### 2. Brand web mentions (15% of GEO pillar)

**What it measures:** the volume and quality of mentions of the customer's brand across the open web, including unlinked mentions. Per Ahrefs 75K-brand study, this is the single strongest predictor of AI Overview citation at r=0.664.

**Method:**

1. The platform queries DataForSEO Backlinks API for the customer's brand name (and registered variants) using the unlinked-mention endpoint.
2. The query returns mention sources, domains, and contextual snippets. The pipeline deduplicates against the customer's owned-domain mentions and filters out obvious noise (forum signature lines, low-quality directory listings).
3. Mention quality is weighted by source domain authority and context relevance. A mention in a Tier 1 publication (TechCrunch, Forbes, industry trade press) is weighted higher than a mention in a low-authority blog.
4. The sub-score is computed as a log-scaled aggregate of weighted mentions over the prior 90 days:

   ```
   brand_mentions_score = log10(weighted_mentions + 1) * scaling_factor
   ```

   Capped at 100. The log scale prevents extreme values (a brand with 100K mentions doesn't get a score of 10,000) while preserving the directional signal.

**Why 15%:** the highest-correlation precursor signal in the 2026 evidence base deserves a substantial weight. 15% is below the AEO pillar's 40% on AIO presence because AI citation is influenced by many factors (mentions are necessary but not sufficient); brand mentions get the highest precursor-signal weight in GEO.

**Data adapter:** new at v0.1. Requires Sprint 2.14 implementation of DataForSEO Backlinks API unlinked-mention queries with quality weighting. Existing backlinks data infrastructure provides the foundation but the unlinked-mention endpoint and quality-weighting logic are new.

### 3. Mention share (10% of GEO pillar)

**What it measures:** the customer's share of mentions across tracked queries compared to designated competitors. This is the "Share of Voice" metric in Peec/Profound terminology, applied to AI search.

**Method:**

1. For each tracked query, the audit pipeline counts mentions of customer and designated competitor brands across the AI engine responses.
2. The mention share for the customer on a given query is `customer_mentions / total_brand_mentions_on_query`.
3. The sub-score aggregates mention share across tracked queries (mean, with confidence flagging for low cohorts).

**Why 10%:** mention share contextualises citation rate against competitive position. It's lower than citation rate because share is a relative measure and the absolute citation outcome is more directly commercially relevant.

### 4. Content freshness (10% of GEO pillar)

**What it measures:** the age and update cadence of the customer's tracked pages.

**Method:**

1. The audit pipeline records `datePublished` and `dateModified` for tracked pages.
2. Freshness is computed per page with a multiplier curve:

   - < 30 days since dateModified → freshness multiplier 1.0
   - 30–90 days → 0.85
   - 90–180 days → 0.65
   - 180–365 days → 0.45
   - > 365 days → 0.30

3. The sub-score aggregates freshness multipliers across tracked pages, weighted by page importance (citation-receiving pages weighted higher than orphan pages).
4. The aggregate is normalised to a 0–100 score.

**Why 10%:** the 2026 evidence base supports a substantial freshness contribution. 3.2× citation multiplier for content < 30 days old is too large to relegate to a hygiene check. The dual-pillar treatment (also weighted modestly in SEO) honours the dual outcome reality.

**Methodology spec:** detailed measurement procedure in `content-freshness/v0.1`.

### 5. Per-platform source affinity (10% of GEO pillar)

**What it measures:** the customer's strength on each AI platform's preferred source types. Reflects the 615× variation in citation patterns across platforms documented by Superlines.

**Method:**

1. For each tier-relevant platform, the audit pipeline measures the customer's presence on that platform's preferred source types:

   - **ChatGPT-preferred:** Wikipedia entity page presence, encyclopedic mentions, well-formed Knowledge Graph signals
   - **Perplexity-preferred:** Reddit mention volume in customer's category subreddits, Quora answer presence
   - **Google AI Overview-preferred:** YouTube channel and video presence, structured video metadata, review-platform citations
   - **Claude-preferred:** blog mentions in topically relevant blogs, expert-authored content references

2. Each platform's affinity score is computed independently using platform-specific signal weights.
3. The sub-score aggregates across tier-relevant platforms with equal weighting between platforms (since the customer's outcome is roughly equally affected by being cited on each platform; weighting by platform commercial-impact is deferred to v0.2).

**Why 10%:** per-platform variation is real and large (615× per Superlines). A flat off-domain measurement misrepresents the citation landscape. 10% recognises this without dominating the pillar — the platform affinity signals matter, but the citation outcome they predict is independently measured.

**Methodology spec:** detailed per-platform measurement in `per-platform-source-affinity/v0.1`.

### 6. Multi-schema + structured-content density (10% of GEO pillar)

**What it measures:** the breadth of schema types deployed on tracked pages plus the density of structured content elements (statistics, quotations, structured Q&A).

**Method:**

1. The audit pipeline parses tracked pages for schema.org markup. Counts distinct schema types per page.
2. The pipeline parses tracked pages for structured content elements:
   - Statistics (regex-detected numerical claims with surrounding context)
   - Quotations (regex-detected quote markers + Haiku-light parse for context)
   - Q&A structure (question-shaped headers with answer paragraphs underneath)
3. The sub-score combines:
   - Schema-type breadth score (pages with 3+ schema types receive full credit; 2 receives 70%; 1 receives 40%; 0 receives 0%)
   - Statistics density score (statistics per 1000 words, capped)
   - Quotations density score (quotations per 1000 words, capped)
4. The aggregate is normalised to 0–100.

**FAQ-structured content is captured here, not in AEO.** Pages with well-formed FAQPage schema and clear Q&A content contribute to the schema-type breadth and Q&A structure components. This is where FAQ-structured content's GEO value lives at v0.1.

**Why 10%:** the 2026 evidence base supports moderate weight. 13% higher citation likelihood for pages with 3+ schema types is meaningful but not dominant. Statistics and quotations multipliers (22% and 37% per Superlines) are real but operate against the same outcome (citation) that's independently measured in sub-score 1.

### 7. AI crawler accessibility (10% of GEO pillar)

**What it measures:** whether the customer's robots.txt and meta robots configuration explicitly grants AI engine crawlers access to the site.

**Method:**

1. The audit pipeline retrieves the customer's robots.txt.
2. For each AI engine crawler (`GPTBot`, `PerplexityBot`, `ClaudeBot`, `Google-Extended`, `cf-think` where applicable, etc.), the pipeline checks:
   - Is the crawler explicitly allowed?
   - Is the crawler explicitly disallowed?
   - Does the crawler fall under default behaviour?
3. The pipeline checks meta robots and X-Robots-Tag headers for `noai`, `noLLM`, `noimageai` directives.
4. The sub-score aggregates per-crawler accessibility into a 0–100 score with each crawler weighted equally within the GEO-relevant set.

**Why 10%:** crawler accessibility is binary in effect (blocked crawlers cannot cite the site) but additive in score (each crawler contributes to the aggregate). It's distinct from runtime compatibility (sub-score 8) because some runtimes do not depend on crawler access (in-context fetch by user-provided URL), while others depend entirely on crawler access (background indexing for citation candidates).

**Methodology spec:** detailed in `ai-crawler-accessibility/v0.1`.

### 8. Runtime compatibility (AI engine runtimes) (15% of GEO pillar)

**What it measures:** whether AI engine runtimes can fetch, parse, and process the customer's site. Computed by routing ARC compatibility findings from runtimes tagged with `relevant_outcome_pillar = 'geo'` or `'multiple'` (with GEO in the routing list).

**Method:**

1. The runtime registry (per `agent-runtime-compatibility/v0.1`) identifies runtimes relevant to GEO. As of May 2026 the principal GEO-relevant runtimes are: Perplexity Fetch, Gemini Fetch, OpenAI Agents SDK web fetch, Claude Agent Skills, AI Overview's citation-side runtime, Cloudflare Project Think (in its GEO-routing role).
2. The audit pipeline executes ARC compatibility checks against each GEO-relevant runtime per the standard ARC pattern.
3. Findings are aggregated:

   ```
   runtime_compat_subscore = (
     count(findings where status = 'compatible' and applicable=true)
     / count(findings where applicable=true)
   ) * 100
   ```

4. Findings are routed to GEO via the runtime's `relevant_outcome_pillar` characteristic.

**Why 15%:** as discussed in the weight rationale, runtime invisibility has more leverage in GEO than in AEO because AI engine runtimes are more heterogeneous. A CSR-only site can fail runtime compatibility with 4 of 6 GEO-relevant runtimes simultaneously. The 15% weight reflects this leverage.

### Note on the Retrievability derived view (cross-pillar)

Two GEO sub-scores — sub-score 7 (AI crawler accessibility) and sub-score 8 (runtime compatibility) — contribute to a cross-cutting derived view called Retrievability. Retrievability combines these with the SEO and AEO pillars' runtime-compatibility sub-scores into a single diagnostic figure that summarises whether the customer's pages are eligible to enter search and AI candidate sets at all.

Retrievability does not contribute to the GEO pillar score; it is computed independently of pillar scoring and is reported separately. However, customers diagnosing GEO sub-score weakness in either sub-score 7 or sub-score 8 should be guided to the retrievability view, which contextualises the failure mode at a higher level — "your pages cannot enter the candidate set in the first place" rather than only "your pages score low on a specific GEO sub-score".

The retrievability view is fully specified in `methodology-overview/v0.1` and in `methodology-adr/v0.1` Decision 15. It is a v0.1 derived view; v0.2 is expected to extend it with server-log fetch evidence — the recommendation in industry coverage (notably Jeffrey, 2026, in Search Engine Land) that retrievability should measure not just whether crawlers are permitted but whether they are actually visiting the customer's pages.

## Aggregation rule

The GEO pillar score is computed as the weighted sum of the eight sub-scores:

```
geo_pillar_score = (
  0.20 * citation_rate_score +
  0.15 * brand_mentions_score +
  0.10 * mention_share_score +
  0.10 * content_freshness_score +
  0.10 * per_platform_affinity_score +
  0.10 * structured_content_score +
  0.10 * ai_crawler_accessibility_score +
  0.15 * runtime_compat_subscore
)
```

Each sub-score is on a 0–100 scale. The pillar score is on a 0–100 scale.

### Independence

The GEO pillar score is independent of SEO and AEO pillar scores. Per `methodology-adr/v0.1` Decision 1, the three pillars measure distinct outcomes and are scored independently.

### Confidence handling

As with AEO, sub-scores with thin data are computed but marked low-confidence. The pillar score honours these flags in customer-facing display. The underlying numeric score is unchanged.

The most common low-confidence cases in GEO at customer onboarding are:

- Citation rate before enough tracked-query cycles have run (typically first 7–14 days)
- Brand mention sub-score for very-early-stage brands (insufficient mention volume to score reliably)
- Per-platform affinity for tiers that don't track all platforms

## Routing of ARC findings into GEO

Per `agent-runtime-compatibility/v0.1`, runtimes in the registry declare a `relevant_outcome_pillar` characteristic. Runtimes tagged `'geo'` route their findings into GEO's runtime sub-score. Runtimes tagged `'multiple'` with GEO in the list route their findings into GEO at the configured share.

The principal GEO-relevant runtimes at v0.1 are:

| Runtime | Pillar routing | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Perplexity Fetch | `geo` | Perplexity-specific fetch architecture; affects GEO citation outcome on Perplexity |
| Gemini Fetch | `geo` | Gemini-specific fetch architecture; affects GEO citation outcome on Gemini |
| OpenAI Agents SDK web fetch | `geo` | ChatGPT/SDK-mediated agent fetch; affects GEO citation outcome on ChatGPT |
| Claude Agent Skills | `geo` | Claude-mediated fetch; affects GEO citation outcome on Claude |
| AI Overview citation-side runtime | `multiple: [aeo, geo]` | AIO is both deterministic answer surface (AEO) and generative citation surface (GEO) |
| Cloudflare Project Think | `multiple: [aeo, geo]` | Project Think mediates both deterministic and generative AI interactions |

The `multiple` routing default (even distribution) means a finding against `cloudflare-project-think` contributes 50% to AEO's runtime sub-score and 50% to GEO's runtime sub-score. Operator-tunable per-runtime routing weights are deferred to v0.2.

## Per-(platform × signal) audit pattern

GEO findings are emitted per (platform × signal) pair for citation and mention measurements, mirroring the per-(check × runtime) pattern in ARC and per-(check × surface) pattern in AEO.

A single query producing AI responses across four engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Gemini) generates findings per engine. Each finding records:

- `platform` — the engine (chatgpt, perplexity, claude, gemini, aio)
- `signal_type` — what was measured (citation, mention, position, etc.)
- `outcome` — the value
- `methodology_ref` — `geo-scoring/v0.1` plus any sub-spec references

This pattern produces customer-facing UI of the form:

> ChatGPT: 23% citation rate, 15% mention share, position 3.2 average
> Perplexity: 18% citation rate, 12% mention share, position 4.1 average
> Claude: 31% citation rate, 22% mention share, position 2.8 average
> Gemini: 14% citation rate, 9% mention share, position 4.6 average

The per-platform breakdown is the primary customer-facing surface for GEO. The aggregate pillar score is the summary; the per-platform detail is where customers diagnose where to focus effort.

## Implementation guidance

### Data adapters

GEO v0.1 requires several new or extended data adapters compared to earlier internal framings:

- **DataForSEO Backlinks API — unlinked mention endpoint.** Required for sub-score 2 (brand web mentions). New adapter at Sprint 2.14.
- **DataForSEO Keywords Data — branded query filter.** Required for branded-search-volume input (informs brand mention quality weighting). Extension of existing adapter at Sprint 2.14.
- **Content freshness measurement.** Page-age tracking with multiplier curve. New repository at Sprint 2.14.
- **Per-platform source affinity adapters.** Reddit mention queries (for Perplexity affinity), Wikipedia entity presence (for ChatGPT affinity), YouTube channel/video presence (for AIO affinity), blog mention queries (for Claude affinity). New adapters at Sprint 2.14.
- **Multi-schema and structured-content density.** Page parsing for schema-type counts, statistics regex, quotations regex, Q&A structure detection. New parser at Sprint 2.14.

### Tracked-query set integration

The tracked-query set is shared with AEO. GEO sub-scores that operate on per-query data (citation rate, mention share, per-platform affinity) use the same query set. The query cap per tier (Starter 50, Growth 200, Scale 500) applies to both pillars.

### Per-platform tier visibility

Tier visibility for GEO sub-scores follows the runtime tier visibility model in ARC. Smaller tiers see fewer platforms in the per-platform breakdown:

- **Starter:** ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AIO (3 platforms)
- **Growth:** Above + Claude, Gemini (5 platforms)
- **Scale:** Above + Cloudflare Project Think mediated interactions (6 platforms)

The pillar score is computed across only the tier-visible platforms. Customers on lower tiers see a smaller per-platform breakdown but the same pillar score formula applies, normalised to their visible platform set.

### Methodology version tagging

Every GEO finding records `methodology_ref = 'geo-scoring/v0.1'`. Sub-score findings may carry dual references to sub-specs (`geo-scoring/v0.1+content-freshness/v0.1`, etc.). When the spec promotes to v0.2, existing findings retain their original version; new findings carry the new version.

### Cascade-monitoring data collection

Per `methodology-adr/v0.1` Deferred 2, GEO is the principal pillar for the v0.2 cascade-weighting review. Implementations must:

- Time-pair per-runtime ARC compatibility findings (from sub-score 8) with per-platform citation outcomes (from sub-score 1)
- Tag customer cohorts by cascade-relevant traits
- Retain findings at the granularity needed for correlation analysis

This is the data infrastructure that feeds the 60-day and 90-day reviews. Without it, the v0.2 cascade-weighting decision has no evidence base.

## Edge cases

### Customer's brand is structurally low-mention

Early-stage brands, brands in niche B2B categories, brands with deliberate stealth positioning — all have structurally low brand web mention volumes. The brand mentions sub-score is computed honestly and flagged low-confidence below a threshold (e.g. < 50 quality-weighted mentions in 90 days). Customer-facing finding explains the limitation. The pillar score formula is unchanged; the customer's path forward is brand-building activity rather than re-scoring.

### Customer's industry has structurally low AI citation rates

Some industries see less AI engagement (some regulated industries, very-specialised B2B niches). The citation rate sub-score reflects this honestly. Customer-facing finding contextualises the score against industry benchmark where benchmark data exists. The pillar score is not adjusted for industry; the customer's AI-era search position is what it is.

### Customer has aggressive AI crawler blocking

A customer who has set `noai` and `noLLM` directives across the site by design (e.g. publishers protecting paywalled content) will score low on AI crawler accessibility (sub-score 7) and likely on citation rate (sub-score 1) by direct consequence. This is correct measurement. Customer-facing finding makes the trade-off explicit; the platform does not advocate removing the blocking.

### Customer's content is genuinely all evergreen

A reference site with stable canonical pages (Wikipedia-like content, dictionaries, glossaries) doesn't update content frequently. The freshness sub-score scores honestly low under the multiplier curve. Customer-facing finding notes that the freshness penalty may be acceptable for genuinely evergreen content; the score is not adjusted. v0.2 review may introduce an "evergreen exemption" for explicitly-tagged stable content if customer feedback warrants.

### CSR site with off-domain authority

A site that fails runtime compatibility (CSR rendering) but has strong off-domain authority (well-established Wikipedia page, extensive third-party coverage) will score:

- Low on runtime compatibility (sub-score 8): ~25 (few runtimes can read the site)
- High on brand web mentions (sub-score 2): ~80 (off-domain mentions are abundant)
- Variable on citation rate (sub-score 1): depends on whether engines cite via training data rather than live retrieval

The pillar score honestly reflects this mixed picture. Cascade weighting (whether the runtime invisibility should *additionally* depress the citation outcomes) is the v0.2 review question.

## Examples

### Example 1: B2B SaaS with strong off-domain authority

A mid-market B2B SaaS company, tracked-query set of 80 queries, strong Wikipedia entity page, frequent industry press coverage.

- Citation rate (across 4 platforms): 28% → `citation_rate_score = 56`
- Brand web mentions (90-day weighted): 320 quality-weighted mentions → `brand_mentions_score = 75`
- Mention share: 18% across tracked queries → `mention_share_score = 36`
- Content freshness: blog updated monthly, key pages updated quarterly → `content_freshness_score = 70`
- Per-platform affinity: strong on Wikipedia (ChatGPT-favourable), moderate on YouTube, weak on Reddit → `affinity_score = 55`
- Schema breadth + structured content: 4 schema types, moderate statistics density → `structured_content_score = 65`
- AI crawler accessibility: all crawlers explicitly allowed → `crawler_accessibility_score = 100`
- Runtime compatibility: SSR site, all GEO runtimes compatible → `runtime_compat_subscore = 100`

GEO pillar score:
```
0.20 * 56 + 0.15 * 75 + 0.10 * 36 + 0.10 * 70 + 0.10 * 55 + 0.10 * 65 + 0.10 * 100 + 0.15 * 100
= 11.2 + 11.25 + 3.6 + 7 + 5.5 + 6.5 + 10 + 15
= 70.05
```

The GEO pillar score is 70. The strongest contributors are brand mentions (15% × 75 = 11.25) and runtime compatibility (15% × 100 = 15). The clearest improvement levers are mention share (currently 36; growing strategic prompt coverage would raise this) and per-platform affinity (currently 55; Reddit and Claude-affinity blog placements would raise this).

### Example 2: Local services business, strong locally but invisible to AI engines

A local services business, tracked-query set of 35 queries, strong Google Business Profile, minimal off-domain mentions.

- Citation rate: 4% (local queries less often cited by AI engines for non-local audiences) → `citation_rate_score = 8`, low-confidence flagged
- Brand web mentions: 22 quality-weighted mentions → `brand_mentions_score = 25`
- Mention share: 5% → `mention_share_score = 10`
- Content freshness: monthly content updates → `content_freshness_score = 75`
- Per-platform affinity: strong on Google AIO (local), weak elsewhere → `affinity_score = 30`
- Schema breadth: LocalBusiness, BreadcrumbList, Organization (3 types) + moderate statistics → `structured_content_score = 60`
- AI crawler accessibility: all crawlers allowed → `crawler_accessibility_score = 100`
- Runtime compatibility: SSR site, all runtimes compatible → `runtime_compat_subscore = 100`

GEO pillar score:
```
0.20 * 8 + 0.15 * 25 + 0.10 * 10 + 0.10 * 75 + 0.10 * 30 + 0.10 * 60 + 0.10 * 100 + 0.15 * 100
= 1.6 + 3.75 + 1 + 7.5 + 3 + 6 + 10 + 15
= 47.85
```

The GEO pillar score is 48. The pillar is dragged down by low citation rate and brand mentions (structural for an early-stage local business with limited off-domain footprint). The runtime and crawler-accessibility sub-scores are at maximum because nothing is technically wrong. The customer's growth path is brand-building activity (off-domain mentions, content marketing, local PR) rather than technical optimisation.

### Example 3: CSR-only React app with strong brand off-domain

A well-established consumer brand with an aggressive CSR architecture, tracked-query set of 100 queries.

- Citation rate: 12% (some engines cite via training data despite runtime invisibility) → `citation_rate_score = 24`
- Brand web mentions: 850 quality-weighted mentions → `brand_mentions_score = 90`
- Mention share: 14% → `mention_share_score = 28`
- Content freshness: marketing pages updated monthly → `content_freshness_score = 70`
- Per-platform affinity: strong off-domain across platforms → `affinity_score = 70`
- Schema breadth: 2 schema types injected by JS, invisible to headless-fetch → effective `structured_content_score = 30` (the schema is there but most runtimes can't see it)
- AI crawler accessibility: all crawlers allowed → `crawler_accessibility_score = 100`
- Runtime compatibility: CSR-only, 1 of 6 runtimes compatible → `runtime_compat_subscore = 17`

GEO pillar score:
```
0.20 * 24 + 0.15 * 90 + 0.10 * 28 + 0.10 * 70 + 0.10 * 70 + 0.10 * 30 + 0.10 * 100 + 0.15 * 17
= 4.8 + 13.5 + 2.8 + 7 + 7 + 3 + 10 + 2.55
= 50.65
```

The GEO pillar score is 51. The site benefits substantially from off-domain authority (brand mentions, affinity) but is held down by runtime invisibility and the schema-not-visible problem that follows from CSR rendering. The clear remediation is server-side rendering of primary content and schema — this would lift the runtime sub-score from 17 to 100 (a 12-point pillar contribution gain) and the structured content sub-score from 30 to a higher value where schema is actually visible to runtimes (another 4–7 point gain).
