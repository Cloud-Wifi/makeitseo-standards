## Abstract

This specification defines how local SEO signals are measured for use as a conditional sub-score in the SEO scoring pillar of the MakeitSEO Standards Programme. Local SEO signals are the specific ranking factors that determine local-pack and Maps inclusion for businesses with location-bound customer reach.

Local SEO is consumed as a conditional sub-score by one pillar: `seo-scoring/v0.1` at 32% weight for local-intent businesses (no contribution for non-local businesses). When a customer is classified as local-intent, the SEO pillar weight distribution shifts to accommodate local SEO signals; the non-local sub-scores reduce proportionally to make space.

The 2026 evidence base for local SEO factor weighting:

- **ClickRank Local SEO Ranking Factors 2026:** six-signal-group model — GBP signals 32%, on-page 19%, reviews 16%, links 15%, behavioural 8%, citation signals 7%
- **SOCi 2026 Local Marketing Report:** confirms GBP signals as the dominant factor
- **Whitespark Local Search Ranking Factors (2024–2026):** longstanding research showing Google Business Profile completeness and review velocity as primary drivers

Local SEO methodology is comparatively well-established. v0.1 codifies the 2026 industry consensus rather than introducing novel methodology.

---

## Status

This is the v0.1 draft of local SEO signals measurement. The signal groups are stable across the industry; v0.2 is expected to be a routine refresh rather than a substantive revision.

The spec is intentionally minimal at v0.1. The principal methodological choices are: (a) which signals to measure, (b) how to weight them within the local sub-score. Both are grounded in the ClickRank 2026 framework.

## Scope

### In scope

- Local-intent classification trigger (which customers receive the local sub-score)
- Six signal groups within local SEO with v0.1 weights
- Measurement methodology for each signal group
- Aggregation rule from per-signal-group scores to the consolidated local sub-score
- Edge cases identified at v0.1

### Out of scope

- Multi-location aggregation strategies for customers operating in multiple markets (treated at the implementation level; not spec'd at v0.1)
- Industry-vertical-specific local signals (e.g. healthcare-specific schema, legal-industry-specific citations; treated as v0.2 extensions)
- Apple Maps and Bing Places signals (Google Business Profile is the dominant local surface in MakeitSEO's customer geography; Apple and Bing are v0.2 candidates)
- Local AI Overview citation (when AIO surfaces in local-intent SERPs; handled by AEO methodology rather than local SEO)

## Definitions

### Local-intent business

A business whose customer reach is geographically bound. In MakeitSEO terms, a business with `service_reach` value in the set `{ regional, metro, local, service-area }` per Wizard Step 3 onboarding. Operator-configurable override is supported for businesses with mixed local and national presence.

### Local pack

The collection of local results (typically three businesses with map markers) displayed prominently in Google search results for local-intent queries. Inclusion in the local pack is the primary commercial outcome local SEO targets.

### Google Business Profile (GBP)

The customer's listing in Google's local-business database, formerly known as Google My Business. GBP is the primary signal source for local pack inclusion.

### NAP consistency

The agreement of Name, Address, Phone across all online listings of the business. Inconsistency (different addresses or phone numbers in different directories) is a negative signal because it suggests entity-disambiguation difficulty for Google.

### Local citation

A mention of the business in a local-relevant directory (Yelp, Yellow Pages, Chamber of Commerce, regional business directories, niche industry directories). Local citations differ from backlinks in that they don't require a hyperlink; presence of consistent NAP data is the signal.

### Review signal

Customer reviews on Google Business Profile, supplemented by reviews on third-party platforms (Yelp, TripAdvisor, niche industry platforms). Review velocity (frequency of new reviews) and review sentiment are both signals.

### Behavioural signal

User-interaction signals captured by Google: click-through rate from the local pack, calls placed via GBP, direction requests, photo-views, message-sends. These signals are not directly observable to operators; they're inferred through aggregate-engagement metadata Google exposes in GBP Insights.

### Local content signal

On-site content optimised for local relevance: location-specific landing pages, service-area pages, locally-themed blog content, embedded maps, location-mentioning microdata.

## Signal groups and weights at v0.1

The implementation measures six signal groups within local SEO, weighted per the 2026 industry consensus:

| Signal group | Weight within local sub-score | What it measures |
|---|---|---|
| GBP signals | 32% | Completeness, categories, photos, posts, hours |
| On-page local content | 19% | Location pages, service-area content, NAP in markup |
| Review signals | 16% | Review volume, velocity, sentiment across platforms |
| Local link signals | 15% | Backlinks from regional and locally-relevant sources |
| Behavioural signals | 8% | Click-through, calls, directions (via GBP Insights) |
| Citation signals | 7% | NAP consistency across directories |

**Total: 100% (within the local sub-score)**

The local sub-score is then weighted at 32% of the SEO pillar for local-intent customers per `seo-scoring/v0.1`.

The signal-group weights are not the only defensible distribution. The ClickRank framework and SOCi report both converge on the values used here. v0.2 may calibrate against cohort data if MakeitSEO's customer outcomes suggest a different weighting.

## Signal group methodology

### 1. GBP signals (32% within local sub-score)

**What it measures:** the completeness and freshness of the customer's Google Business Profile.

**Method:**

1. The implementation queries the Google Business Profile API for the customer's GBP listing.
2. Completeness components measured:
   - Business name correctness (matches customer's canonical name)
   - Address presence and validity
   - Phone number presence
   - Website link presence
   - Hours of operation completeness (including special hours for holidays where relevant)
   - Primary category accuracy
   - Additional categories (up to 9 supplementary)
   - Service / product list completeness
   - Photo count (interior, exterior, products, team) — minimum 8 photos for full credit
   - GBP post recency (posts within 30 days for full credit)
3. Each component contributes to a 0–100 GBP completeness score; the weighted aggregate is the GBP signal group score.

**Data adapter requirement:** Google Business Profile API integration. Existing platform infrastructure may need extension; documented in implementation guidance.

### 2. On-page local content (19% within local sub-score)

**What it measures:** the customer's on-site content optimisation for local relevance.

**Method:**

1. For each tracked page, the implementation parses for local-relevance signals:
   - LocalBusiness schema markup with accurate NAP
   - Location names in page titles, meta descriptions, H1s, H2s
   - Service-area pages (per geography served, where applicable)
   - Embedded Google Maps or location-rendering markup
   - Internal-link patterns consistent with location-pages-as-hub
2. The signal group score is an aggregate across tracked pages.

### 3. Review signals (16% within local sub-score)

**What it measures:** the customer's review profile across platforms.

**Method:**

1. The implementation captures review data from:
   - Google Business Profile reviews (via GBP API)
   - Operator-configured third-party platforms relevant to the customer's industry (Yelp, TripAdvisor, niche platforms)
2. Three sub-components within the review signal:
   - **Review volume** — total review count across platforms, log-scaled (similar to brand mentions normalisation)
   - **Review velocity** — review count over the prior 90 days; recent activity weighted more than historical
   - **Review sentiment** — average star rating across platforms, weighted by review count
3. Aggregate review signal score = `0.30 × volume_score + 0.40 × velocity_score + 0.30 × sentiment_score` (illustrative weights; final calibration in implementation reference)

### 4. Local link signals (15% within local sub-score)

**What it measures:** backlinks from regional and locally-relevant sources.

**Method:**

1. The implementation queries DataForSEO Backlinks API for the customer's backlink profile, filtered by source-domain location and category:
   - Local Chamber of Commerce, business associations, neighbourhood networks
   - Regional press and trade publications
   - Local non-profit and community organisation sites
   - Industry-vertical directories specific to the customer's service area
2. Locally-relevant backlinks weighted higher than generic high-DA backlinks for this signal group.
3. Aggregate local link score = log-scaled weighted count.

Note: this signal group is distinct from the general SEO pillar's Authority signals sub-score (which captures all backlinks regardless of locality). Local link signals are a subset; the local sub-score weights them by local-relevance.

### 5. Behavioural signals (8% within local sub-score)

**What it measures:** user-interaction signals from Google Business Profile Insights.

**Method:**

1. The implementation queries GBP Insights API for:
   - Search impressions (how often the business appeared in local results)
   - Profile views (click-through to the GBP listing)
   - Website clicks (click-through to the customer's domain)
   - Direction requests (users requesting driving directions)
   - Phone calls (call clicks)
   - Photo views
2. The signal group score aggregates these into a 0–100 score, normalised against industry-vertical benchmarks (operator-configured) so that a typical mid-market customer scores around 50.

### 6. Citation signals (7% within local sub-score)

**What it measures:** NAP consistency across local-business directories.

**Method:**

1. The implementation queries DataForSEO and supplementary directory APIs for the customer's listings across:
   - Yelp, Yellow Pages, BBB, Foursquare, niche industry directories (operator-configured per customer industry)
2. For each listing, the implementation extracts the listed Name, Address, Phone.
3. NAP consistency score = `(consistent_listings / total_listings) × 100`, where "consistent" means exact match (after normalisation: removing punctuation, standardising address abbreviations, etc.) with the canonical NAP from GBP.
4. Findings emitted for each inconsistent listing with a recommended fix.

## Aggregation rule

The local sub-score is the weighted sum of the six signal groups:

```
local_sub_score = (
  0.32 × gbp_signals_score +
  0.19 × on_page_local_content_score +
  0.16 × review_signals_score +
  0.15 × local_link_signals_score +
  0.08 × behavioural_signals_score +
  0.07 × citation_signals_score
)
```

The result is a 0–100 score. This score is then weighted at 32% of the SEO pillar per `seo-scoring/v0.1`.

## Local-intent classification

A customer is treated as local-intent if any of the following apply:

1. `business_contexts.service_reach` is in the set `{ regional, metro, local, service-area }`
2. The operator has manually flagged the customer as local-intent (override)

For non-local customers, the local sub-score is not computed and does not contribute to the SEO pillar score; the non-local SEO weight distribution per `seo-scoring/v0.1` applies.

Mixed-presence customers (customers with both national reach and physical locations) are treated per the operator's primary classification. v0.2 may introduce a multi-classification framework where the SEO pillar score is computed as a weighted blend of local and non-local methodologies; v0.1 supports operator override but not blended scoring.

## Measurement procedure

### Cadence

Local SEO measurement runs as part of each audit cycle. Per-signal-group cadences:

- **GBP signals:** refreshed weekly (signal evolves with operator activity on GBP)
- **On-page local content:** measured per audit (changes with site updates)
- **Review signals:** refreshed weekly (review velocity matters)
- **Local link signals:** measured per audit; data adapter has its own monthly refresh
- **Behavioural signals:** refreshed weekly (GBP Insights have daily granularity)
- **Citation signals:** refreshed monthly (NAP consistency changes infrequently)

### Methodology version tagging

Every local SEO measurement carries `methodology_ref = 'local-seo-signals/v0.1'`. Findings consumed by SEO pillar scoring may carry dual references (`local-seo-signals/v0.1+seo-scoring/v0.1`).

### Data adapter dependencies

Local SEO measurement requires several data adapters beyond the core platform:

- **Google Business Profile API:** required for GBP signals and behavioural signals. The customer authorises the platform to access their GBP via OAuth during onboarding.
- **Third-party review platform APIs:** operator-configured per customer industry (Yelp Fusion API, TripAdvisor API, etc.)
- **Directory citation scrapers:** operator-maintained for the principal directory sites in the customer's market

Implementations that cannot provide GBP API access (e.g. customers without a verified GBP listing) cannot fully compute the local sub-score. v0.1 handles this with a partial-computation path: the available signal groups contribute proportionally to the sub-score; the customer-facing finding clearly identifies which signal groups are unavailable and recommends remediation.

## Implementation guidance

### GBP authorisation

The customer must authorise the platform to access their Google Business Profile during onboarding. The authorisation flow is OAuth-based; specifications for the OAuth scope and refresh-token handling are documented in the implementation reference.

For customers managing multiple GBP locations (e.g. agencies managing locations on behalf of clients), the authorisation flow is per-location. v0.1 supports multi-location GBP measurement; the aggregation rule for multi-location customers is per the operator's primary-location classification.

### Industry-vertical directory configuration

The operator maintains industry-vertical-specific directory lists per customer industry. v0.1 ships with starter lists for the principal MakeitSEO customer industries (hospitality, professional services, healthcare, retail, B2B services). Customers in industries not covered receive a default directory list plus a flag for operator review.

### Local-intent override UI

The operator can flag a customer as local-intent regardless of `service_reach`. This is an admin route; the customer does not toggle it directly. Use cases:

- National chains with strong local-pack dependency (e.g. a national restaurant brand whose competitive position is local for most customers)
- Customers whose `service_reach` is recorded incorrectly during onboarding
- Customers operating across local and national channels with the local channel being the operator's priority focus

## Edge cases

### Customer has no Google Business Profile

A customer without a verified GBP has a structural gap in local SEO measurement. v0.1 handles this with a partial-computation path:

- GBP signals score = 0 (or "data unavailable" with explanation)
- Behavioural signals score = 0 (GBP Insights unavailable)
- Other signal groups computed normally
- Aggregate local sub-score reflects the gap honestly

The customer-facing finding is the strongest possible nudge to set up a GBP listing.

### Customer's GBP is unverified

An unverified GBP is partially accessible. v0.1 captures the available data (basic listing presence, completeness of optional fields) but flags the verification gap. Verification status is captured in the GBP signals score as a multiplier — unverified listings receive ~50% credit on the otherwise-applicable signal score.

### Multi-location customers

A customer with multiple physical locations (e.g. a retail chain with stores in multiple cities) requires per-location measurement. v0.1 supports this:

- Each location is measured independently per the local sub-score methodology
- The customer's overall local sub-score is the weighted mean across locations
- Weights are operator-configurable (per-location traffic, per-location revenue, equal weighting); equal weighting is the v0.1 default

### Customer's directory citations are intentionally limited

Some businesses deliberately limit their directory presence (e.g. boutique professional services that prefer referrals over directory traffic). v0.1 doesn't adjust for this; the citation signals score is honestly low. The customer-facing finding explains the trade-off; the operator may flag specific customers as "directory-light by design" in the admin route to suppress the recommendation.

### Customer's reviews are predominantly negative

A customer with predominantly negative reviews has a low review sentiment score, an honestly low review signal group score, and consequently a lower local sub-score. v0.1 surfaces the sentiment honestly without recommending review manipulation. The customer-facing finding may suggest review-response best practices but does not artificially adjust the score.

### Customer operates in a foreign-language market

Local SEO measurement works in any language as long as the underlying data adapters support the market. v0.1's English-market support is most complete; non-English markets may have partial directory coverage. Implementations should flag market-coverage limitations in the customer-facing finding.

## Examples

### Example 1: Established local services business

A dental practice in the customer's primary market. Mature GBP, regular reviews, consistent NAP.

- GBP signals: 88 (complete profile, regular posts, 32 photos)
- On-page local content: 75 (location page, service-area page, schema markup)
- Review signals: 82 (145 reviews, 4.6 average, 6 new in last 30 days)
- Local link signals: 68 (Chamber of Commerce, regional press, 2 niche directories)
- Behavioural signals: 71 (above-average click-through, calls)
- Citation signals: 91 (consistent NAP across 14 directories)

Local sub-score:
```
0.32 × 88 + 0.19 × 75 + 0.16 × 82 + 0.15 × 68 + 0.08 × 71 + 0.07 × 91
= 28.16 + 14.25 + 13.12 + 10.20 + 5.68 + 6.37
= 77.78
```

Local sub-score is 78. Strong across the board with citation signals (91) the highest contributor relative to weight; the 32% GBP weight produces the largest absolute contribution (28.16). Clearest improvement levers: local link signals (currently 68; partnering with more regional publications would raise this) and behavioural signals (71; growing GBP completeness drives engagement).

### Example 2: New local business

A new restaurant launched 3 months ago. GBP verified but new; few reviews; minimal directory presence.

- GBP signals: 65 (verified, basic profile, 4 photos, no posts yet)
- On-page local content: 40 (basic site, no schema, no service-area pages)
- Review signals: 30 (12 reviews, 4.4 average, but few in last 30 days)
- Local link signals: 22 (no notable backlinks yet)
- Behavioural signals: 35 (low search impressions, modest engagement)
- Citation signals: 50 (listed in 4 directories with consistent NAP)

Local sub-score:
```
0.32 × 65 + 0.19 × 40 + 0.16 × 30 + 0.15 × 22 + 0.08 × 35 + 0.07 × 50
= 20.80 + 7.60 + 4.80 + 3.30 + 2.80 + 3.50
= 42.80
```

Local sub-score is 43. Honest reflection of early-stage status. The growth path is GBP completion (photos, posts) → review velocity → local press → directory citation expansion, over 6–12 months.

### Example 3: National chain with local presence

A national restaurant brand with a flagship local location, operator-flagged as local-intent.

- GBP signals: 92 (mature, complete, frequent posts)
- On-page local content: 85 (location-specific landing pages, schema)
- Review signals: 75 (broad review base, regular velocity)
- Local link signals: 81 (national brand authority plus regional press)
- Behavioural signals: 78
- Citation signals: 89 (consistent NAP across many directories)

Local sub-score:
```
0.32 × 92 + 0.19 × 85 + 0.16 × 75 + 0.15 × 81 + 0.08 × 78 + 0.07 × 89
= 29.44 + 16.15 + 12.00 + 12.15 + 6.24 + 6.23
= 82.21
```

Local sub-score is 82. The national brand authority helps the local link signals; per-location GBP investment produces strong behavioural signals. The customer-facing finding may surface "your national authority is strong; the principal local opportunity is review velocity at this specific location" or similar.
