## Abstract

This specification defines the measurement methodology for the AEO (Answer Engine Optimisation) pillar in the MakeitSEO Standards Programme. AEO measures inclusion in **deterministic answer surfaces** — featured snippets, AI Overviews, knowledge panels, People Also Ask, and related structured answer surfaces. Inclusion in these surfaces is the customer outcome AEO tracks.

AEO is one of three outcome pillars in the MakeitSEO Standards Programme, alongside SEO (traditional ranking) and GEO (generative AI citation). The three pillars compose to the overall MakeitSEO score as the arithmetic mean of their independent pillar scores. The architectural reasoning is captured in `methodology-adr/v0.1`.

AEO v0.1 is distinct from earlier draft framings in two material ways:

1. **FAQ schema and HowTo schema are no longer AEO signals.** Following Google's 7 May 2026 deprecation of FAQ rich results for all sites, the rich-result outcomes these schema types historically produced no longer exist for the vast majority of sites. FAQ and HowTo signal weight is removed from AEO entirely; the freed weight redistributes across surviving sub-scores. Where FAQ-structured content remains useful for AI citation, it is captured in the GEO pillar via `geo-scoring/v0.1` structured-content sub-scoring.
2. **AI Overview presence is elevated to the dominant AEO surface.** With 50%+ of Google searches now showing AI Overviews (up from 18% in March 2025), AIO is the principal answer surface customers can earn inclusion in. The sub-score weight reflects this commercial reality.

A runtime-compatibility sub-score routes ARC findings into AEO per `agent-runtime-compatibility/v0.1` Decision 3. This sub-score covers answer-surface fetchers (Google AIO pipeline, Bing Chat surface, deterministic snippet fetchers).

## Definitions

### Answer engine

A search-engine or AI-engine surface that produces a direct answer rather than (or in addition to) a ranked list of links. As of May 2026 the principal deterministic answer surfaces are: Google featured snippets, Google AI Overviews, Google knowledge panels, Google People Also Ask, Bing answer surfaces (including Bing Chat answer presentation), regional search engines' answer features.

The distinguishing property of an *answer engine* surface (as opposed to a *generative AI citation* surface) is that inclusion is rule-driven rather than probabilistic. A site that satisfies the structural and content requirements for featured snippet eligibility will, given sufficient ranking authority, capture the snippet for relevant queries. A site cited by ChatGPT or Perplexity is selected by probabilistic retrieval from a much larger candidate set. The two are measured separately and constitute the AEO and GEO pillars respectively.

### Deterministic answer surface

A search-result feature that, when populated, displays a structured answer or entity summary derived from a specific page or set of pages. The display is selected by a deterministic process driven by structured signals on the source pages plus the query. Featured snippets, AI Overviews, knowledge panels, and PAA boxes are deterministic answer surfaces. (AI Overviews are partly deterministic and partly generative; for AEO purposes we measure the deterministic inclusion outcome — whether the customer's content was cited in the AIO — rather than the generative composition.)

### AEO outcome

A measured event where a customer's content appears in a deterministic answer surface for a tracked query. The outcome may be: featured snippet captured, AI Overview citation included, knowledge panel populated with the customer's information, PAA box including the customer's content. Each outcome event is tracked per query, per surface, per timestamp.

### AEO sub-score

One of the five components of the AEO pillar score at v0.1. Each sub-score measures a specific dimension of AEO performance with an explicit weight that sums to 100% across sub-scores.

### Pillar runtime sub-score

The runtime-compatibility component of the AEO sub-score set, populated by routing ARC findings from runtimes tagged with `relevant_outcome_pillar = 'aeo'` (or `'multiple'` with AEO in the routing list). Specified in `agent-runtime-compatibility/v0.1`.

## Sub-scores and weights

AEO v0.1 defines five sub-scores. Weights are evidence-grounded against the 2026 research base (HubSpot AEO framework, AirOps measurement, ALM Corp positioning, Conductor benchmarks) and the principle of weighting toward commercial impact.

| Sub-score | Weight | What it measures |
|---|---|---|
| AI Overview presence | 40% | Inclusion in Google AI Overviews and equivalent AI-generated answer surfaces |
| Featured snippet capture | 25% | Capture rate for featured snippet positions across tracked queries |
| Knowledge panel signals | 15% | Entity disambiguation, knowledge graph presence, knowledge panel population |
| People Also Ask presence | 10% | Inclusion in PAA boxes for tracked queries |
| Runtime compatibility (answer-surface fetchers) | 10% | Whether answer-surface fetchers can read the site, routed from ARC |

**Total: 100%**

The weight distribution reflects three judgements:

1. **AI Overviews now dominate AEO surface inclusion.** With 50%+ of Google searches showing AIO (up from 18% in March 2025), AIO is the principal answer surface. Pre-AIO AEO frameworks weighted featured snippets at 25–35%; the 2026 reality is AIO has absorbed much of that weight.
2. **Featured snippets remain commercially valuable.** They have not been displaced as much as some 2024-era predictions suggested. ChatGPT referrals convert at 15.9% vs Google Organic at 1.76% (Seer Interactive), but featured-snippet click-through still drives meaningful traffic for queries where AIO doesn't appear.
3. **Knowledge panels and PAA are smaller but real contributors.** Knowledge panels matter for branded queries and local. PAA boxes have grown in coverage but contribute less to direct outcomes than featured snippets.

The 10% runtime sub-score is intentionally modest. Most AEO failures are not runtime failures — they are structural content failures (no answer-shaped content, no schema, weak entity signals) that affect AEO regardless of runtime. The runtime sub-score captures the small but real cases where a site is structurally AEO-ready but is invisible to answer-surface fetchers because of CSR rendering or crawler blocking.

### Removed from AEO v0.1

The following sub-scores were present in earlier (internal) draft framings and have been removed:

- **FAQ schema coverage.** Removed following Google's 7 May 2026 deprecation of FAQ rich results for all sites. FAQ rich results — the SERP outcome that justified the original weight — no longer exist for ~99% of sites. FAQ-structured content remains useful for AI citation and is captured in `geo-scoring/v0.1` under structured-content sub-scoring. See Background section below for the deprecation context.
- **HowTo schema coverage.** Removed for the same reason (HowTo rich results fully deprecated September 2023 desktop and never returned). Captured in `geo-scoring/v0.1` if applicable.

The freed weight (30% combined from FAQ + HowTo) redistributed across the surviving sub-scores per the table above.

## Sub-score methodology

### 1. AI Overview presence (40% of AEO pillar)

**What it measures:** the rate at which the customer's domain appears as a cited source within Google AI Overviews for tracked queries. Optionally extends to equivalent surfaces (Bing Chat, regional AI-overview surfaces) if measurement infrastructure exists.

**Method:**

1. The platform maintains a tracked-query set per customer. Composition: customer-confirmed strategic queries + auto-suggested queries discovered during competitor analysis. Tier-gated count per pricing tier (Starter 50, Growth 200, Scale 500 per the established tier limits).
2. For each tracked query, the audit pipeline triggers a Google query via DataForSEO's SERP API with AI Overview detection enabled.
3. The pipeline records:
   - Whether an AI Overview appears for the query
   - If yes, whether the customer's domain is cited among the AIO sources
   - The citation position within the AIO (which source-card position)
4. The sub-score is computed as:

   ```
   aio_presence_score = (
     (queries_with_customer_in_aio / queries_where_aio_appears) * 100
   )
   ```

   With epistemic floors: if `queries_where_aio_appears` is < 20, the score is computed but marked as `low_confidence = true` in the finding. Customers see a "limited data" badge until cohort size is sufficient.

**Why 40%:** AIO is the principal answer surface in 2026. The strongest correlation between AEO investment and downstream commercial outcomes runs through AIO inclusion.

### 2. Featured snippet capture (25% of AEO pillar)

**What it measures:** the rate at which the customer's content captures the featured snippet position for tracked queries that show a featured snippet.

**Method:**

1. For each tracked query, the audit pipeline records whether a featured snippet appears in the SERP and, if yes, the domain of the source.
2. The sub-score is computed as:

   ```
   snippet_capture_score = (
     (queries_with_customer_snippet / queries_where_snippet_appears) * 100
   )
   ```

   With the same low-confidence flag as AIO presence (< 20 queries → marked).

3. Findings note whether the customer's snippet is at risk of being displaced by AIO (when both appear, the AIO typically reduces snippet click-through significantly).

**Why 25%:** featured snippets remain commercially valuable for the queries where AIO does not appear (still ~50% of searches as of May 2026). Their share of AEO weight is significant but secondary to AIO.

### 3. Knowledge panel signals (15% of AEO pillar)

**What it measures:** the strength of the customer's entity presence in Google's knowledge graph as evidenced by knowledge panel population and entity disambiguation.

**Method:**

1. The audit pipeline checks for the presence of a knowledge panel for the customer's primary entity (organisation name, person name for personal brands, product name for product-led brands) on branded queries.
2. If a knowledge panel is present, the pipeline records its completeness: photo present, description present, founding date, key people, social profiles, related entities, etc.
3. If no knowledge panel is present, the pipeline records the entity-signal precursors: Organization schema validity on the site, sameAs links to known entity databases (Wikipedia, Wikidata, Crunchbase, LinkedIn Company), Google Business Profile presence (where applicable), entity-disambiguation strength (whether the customer's brand resolves unambiguously vs colliding with other entities sharing the name).
4. The sub-score combines knowledge panel completeness (if present) with entity-signal precursor strength (if not present). Computation details specified in the implementation reference.

**Why 15%:** entity authority is a precondition for AEO inclusion in branded and semi-branded queries. A site with weak entity signals struggles across all AEO surfaces because Google doesn't reliably resolve the brand as a distinct entity.

### 4. People Also Ask presence (10% of AEO pillar)

**What it measures:** the rate at which the customer's content appears in PAA boxes for tracked queries that show PAA.

**Method:**

1. For each tracked query, the audit pipeline records whether PAA appears and which domains are cited in PAA answer expansions.
2. The sub-score is computed as:

   ```
   paa_presence_score = (
     (queries_with_customer_in_paa / queries_where_paa_appears) * 100
   )
   ```

3. PAA presence is weighted lower than featured snippets because PAA citations drive less direct traffic and less direct commercial outcome than snippet captures.

**Why 10%:** PAA boxes have broad coverage but lower per-citation commercial impact than featured snippets or AIO. Important to track, less weight in the score.

### 5. Runtime compatibility (answer-surface fetchers) (10% of AEO pillar)

**What it measures:** whether answer-surface fetchers can read the customer's site. Computed by routing ARC compatibility findings from runtimes tagged with `relevant_outcome_pillar = 'aeo'` or `'multiple'` (with AEO in the routing list).

**Method:**

1. The runtime registry (per `agent-runtime-compatibility/v0.1`) identifies runtimes relevant to AEO. As of May 2026 the principal AEO-relevant runtimes are: Google AIO fetcher pipeline (which inherits Googlebot's rendering capabilities), Bing Chat answer-surface fetcher, deterministic snippet fetchers.
2. The audit pipeline executes the ARC compatibility checks against each AEO-relevant runtime per the standard ARC pattern.
3. Findings are aggregated per the formula:

   ```
   runtime_compat_subscore = (
     count(findings where status = 'compatible' and applicable=true)
     / count(findings where applicable=true)
   ) * 100
   ```

4. Findings are routed to AEO via the runtime's `relevant_outcome_pillar` characteristic per ARC v0.1.

**Why 10%:** runtime invisibility for AEO surfaces is real but most AEO failures are content-structural, not runtime-structural. The 10% weight ensures that runtime invisibility produces a measurable depression in the AEO score without dominating it. Cascade weighting (whether ARC failures should *amplify* into AEO beyond the sub-score effect) is a v0.2 review question per `methodology-adr/v0.1` Deferred 2.

## Aggregation rule

The AEO pillar score is computed as the weighted sum of the five sub-scores:

```
aeo_pillar_score = (
  0.40 * aio_presence_score +
  0.25 * snippet_capture_score +
  0.15 * knowledge_panel_score +
  0.10 * paa_presence_score +
  0.10 * runtime_compat_subscore
)
```

Each sub-score is on a 0–100 scale. The pillar score is on a 0–100 scale.

### Independence

The AEO pillar score is independent of SEO and GEO pillar scores. Per `methodology-adr/v0.1` Decision 1, the three pillars measure distinct outcomes and are scored independently. The overall MakeitSEO score composes them as the arithmetic mean.

### Confidence handling

Sub-scores with low data (e.g. < 20 queries showing AIO) are computed but marked `low_confidence = true`. The pillar score honours these confidence flags in customer-facing display: a pillar score with any low-confidence sub-score shows a "limited data" badge with explanation. The underlying numeric score is unchanged; only the display annotates the confidence state.

## Routing of ARC findings into AEO

Per `agent-runtime-compatibility/v0.1`, runtimes in the registry declare a `relevant_outcome_pillar` characteristic. Runtimes tagged `'aeo'` route their findings into AEO's runtime sub-score. Runtimes tagged `'multiple'` with AEO in the list route their findings into AEO at the configured share (default: even distribution across listed pillars).

The principal AEO-relevant runtimes at v0.1 are:

| Runtime | Pillar routing | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Google AIO fetcher pipeline | `multiple: [aeo, geo]` | AIO is both a deterministic answer surface (AEO) and a generative citation surface (GEO) |
| Bing Chat answer-surface fetcher | `multiple: [aeo, geo]` | Similar dual nature |
| Deterministic snippet fetcher (Googlebot rendering for featured snippets) | `multiple: [seo, aeo]` | Snippets are part of SEO ranking flow and AEO inclusion |

Note: as of v0.1 the runtime registry's `relevant_outcome_pillar = 'multiple'` defaults to even distribution. Operator-tunable per-runtime routing weights are deferred to v0.2.

## Per-(check × surface) audit pattern

AEO findings are emitted per (check × surface) pair, mirroring the per-(check × runtime) pattern in ARC v0.1.

Where an AEO check evaluates against multiple surfaces (e.g. "structured content for definitions" applies to featured snippets, AIO citations, and PAA boxes), the audit emits one finding per surface. The findings table carries a `surface_id` field.

This pattern produces customer-facing UI of the form:

> Your site captures the featured snippet for 47% of tracked queries.
> Your site is cited in AI Overviews for 12% of tracked queries.
> Your knowledge panel is present and 80% complete.
> Your site appears in PAA for 23% of tracked queries.

Each line of the breakdown is a per-surface aggregation of findings, mirroring ARC's runtime breakdown pattern.

## Background — why FAQ and HowTo are not AEO signals at v0.1

This section is informational. It exists to explain the decision and to prevent re-introduction of FAQ/HowTo as AEO sub-scores in future revisions without explicit reversal.

### The historical framing

Pre-2023 SEO methodology treated FAQ schema and HowTo schema as primary AEO signals. The reasoning was straightforward: both schema types produced visible rich-result outcomes in Google SERPs — the FAQ dropdown of expandable Q&A under a result; the HowTo numbered-step display.

A 2020-era AEO scoring framework reasonably weighted FAQ schema coverage at 15–25% and HowTo coverage at 5–10%.

### What changed

In August 2023, Google restricted FAQ rich results to government and health sites. For ~99% of sites, the FAQ rich result was effectively gone; the schema markup remained valid but produced no SERP feature.

HowTo rich results were fully deprecated on desktop in September 2023.

On 7 May 2026, Google announced that FAQ rich results would no longer appear in Google Search for any category of website. The search appearance, rich result report in Search Console, and Rich Results Test support are scheduled for removal in June 2026. Search Console API support for FAQ rich result data follows in August 2026.

The deprecation completed a process that began in 2023. By May 2026, FAQ rich results were not appearing for almost any site.

### Why this matters for AEO scoring

The AEO sub-score for FAQ schema coverage was measuring a contribution to a SERP outcome (the FAQ rich result) that no longer exists for the vast majority of sites. Continuing to score FAQ schema as an AEO signal would mean awarding score points for a contribution that produces no measurable outcome.

This is the definition of a methodology error.

### Where FAQ-structured content remains relevant

FAQ schema remains valid markup. Google has stated it may continue to use FAQ structured data to better understand pages even without rendering a rich result. AI engines parse structured Q&A content and pages with multiple schema types have a 13% higher likelihood of being cited by LLMs (per 2026 State of AI Search).

FAQ-structured content is therefore captured in the GEO pillar via `geo-scoring/v0.1` under structured-content sub-scoring, at modest weight, as one of several signals affecting AI citation probability. It is not an AEO signal.

### Reversal criteria

If FAQ rich results are reintroduced by Google as a visible SERP feature for general sites in a future update, this decision will be revisited. The default position at any future review is to retain the current AEO scoring (FAQ excluded) unless Google's deprecation is materially reversed.

## Implementation guidance

### Tracked-query set composition

The tracked-query set is the foundation of AEO measurement. Implementations should:

- Confirm customer-strategic queries during onboarding (Wizard Step 5 — Goals & Competitors per the platform's onboarding flow)
- Auto-suggest additional queries during competitor analysis (queries where competitors are appearing in AEO surfaces but customer is not)
- Cap the tracked-query set per pricing tier (Starter 50, Growth 200, Scale 500)
- Refresh the tracked-query set monthly or on material business-context change

### DataForSEO integration

The principal data adapter for AEO surface detection is DataForSEO's SERP API with AI Overview detection enabled. Implementations should:

- Schedule queries via the standard audit cadence per pricing tier
- Record full SERP response payloads (not just verdicts) so post-hoc analysis can refine the AEO methodology without re-querying
- Tag responses with the AEO scoring methodology version (`aeo-scoring/v0.1`) for forward compatibility

### Pillar runtime sub-score integration

The runtime sub-score is computed by the ARC subsystem and routed to AEO. Implementations should not duplicate ARC logic in the AEO scorer; they should subscribe to ARC findings tagged for AEO and aggregate per the formula above.

### Low-confidence handling

When data is thin (< 20 queries showing a given surface), the sub-score is computed but the finding carries `low_confidence = true`. The implementation must:

- Honour the flag in customer-facing display (badge, tooltip, or equivalent)
- Avoid quoting the score as a definitive number when low-confidence
- Suggest additional tracked queries to grow cohort size

### Methodology version tagging

Every AEO finding records `methodology_ref = 'aeo-scoring/v0.1'`. When the spec promotes to v0.2 (likely with revised weights based on first-cohort calibration), existing findings retain their original version; new findings carry the new version.

## Edge cases

### Customer has no tracked queries

A customer in the pre-onboarding state has no tracked queries. The AEO pillar score is computed as `null` rather than 0, and customer-facing UI displays "AEO measurement begins after onboarding completion." The runtime sub-score is independently measurable and is computed from launch; the four content-driven sub-scores require tracked queries.

### Customer's AIO presence is structurally zero (B2B SaaS targeting non-AIO queries)

Some customer query sets show AIO infrequently (some B2B SaaS verticals, niche professional services). The AEO sub-score for AIO presence is calculated only on queries where AIO appears; if the denominator is near-zero, the sub-score is flagged low-confidence.

For such customers, the featured snippet and knowledge panel sub-scores carry more practical weight. Customer-facing UI should explain this without changing the spec's weight rule. The 40/25/15/10/10 weights are fixed in v0.1.

### AIO vs featured snippet displacement

For queries where both AIO and featured snippet appear, the snippet click-through is significantly reduced by AIO. The AEO scoring does not directly account for displacement (it counts both as separate sub-scores). Implementations should surface the displacement information in customer-facing finding details ("your snippet for query X is at risk because AIO now appears for that query") without changing the pillar score formula.

### Knowledge panel for ambiguous entity names

A customer whose brand collides with a more famous entity (e.g. a small SaaS named "Apple") has structurally limited knowledge panel options. The knowledge panel sub-score is computed honestly; customer-facing finding explains the entity-collision issue. The spec does not adjust the weight for collision cases.

### Regional and language variations

AEO measurement is region-aware. The audit pipeline runs queries with the customer's target locale, country, and language. Cross-region measurement (a customer with both UK and US presence) requires per-region tracked-query sets and per-region AEO sub-scores; the pillar score is aggregated as a customer-weighted mean of regional pillar scores. Implementation pattern specified in the implementation reference.

## Examples

### Example 1: B2B SaaS with strong AIO coverage

A B2B SaaS company with strong content authority on its category, tracked-query set of 80 queries.

- AIO appears for 65 of 80 queries; customer is cited in 18 of those: `aio_presence_score = 27.7`
- Featured snippet appears for 45 of 80 queries; customer captures 12: `snippet_capture_score = 26.7`
- Knowledge panel present, 60% complete: `knowledge_panel_score = 60`
- PAA appears for 70 of 80 queries; customer in 14: `paa_presence_score = 20`
- Runtime compat: SSR site, all AEO-relevant runtimes compatible: `runtime_compat_subscore = 100`

AEO pillar score:
```
0.40 * 27.7 + 0.25 * 26.7 + 0.15 * 60 + 0.10 * 20 + 0.10 * 100
= 11.08 + 6.675 + 9 + 2 + 10
= 38.76
```

The AEO pillar score is 39. The strongest lever is AIO presence (which has the highest weight) — going from 27.7 to 50 on AIO presence alone would raise the pillar score by 9 points.

### Example 2: Local services business with strong knowledge panel

A local services business with strong Google Business Profile, tracked-query set of 35 queries.

- AIO appears for 8 of 35 queries (local queries less likely to show AIO); customer in 2: `aio_presence_score = 25`, flagged low-confidence (n < 20)
- Featured snippet appears for 22 of 35; customer captures 8: `snippet_capture_score = 36.4`
- Knowledge panel present, 95% complete (strong GBP, multiple sameAs): `knowledge_panel_score = 95`
- PAA appears for 28 of 35; customer in 9: `paa_presence_score = 32.1`
- Runtime compat: SSR with all runtimes compatible: `runtime_compat_subscore = 100`

AEO pillar score:
```
0.40 * 25 + 0.25 * 36.4 + 0.15 * 95 + 0.10 * 32.1 + 0.10 * 100
= 10 + 9.1 + 14.25 + 3.21 + 10
= 46.56
```

The AEO pillar score is 47. The knowledge panel contribution is significant; the AIO presence sub-score is low-confidence-flagged so the customer sees the explanation. The clearest improvement levers are featured snippet capture (currently 36.4) and PAA presence (currently 32.1).

### Example 3: CSR-only React app with AIO invisibility

A React SPA marketing site that renders content client-side, tracked-query set of 60 queries.

- AIO appears for 35 of 60 queries; customer never cited (AIO fetcher doesn't render JS): `aio_presence_score = 0`
- Featured snippet appears for 25 of 60; customer never captures: `snippet_capture_score = 0`
- Knowledge panel present (entity well-established off-domain), 70% complete: `knowledge_panel_score = 70`
- PAA appears for 40 of 60; customer never in: `paa_presence_score = 0`
- Runtime compat: CSR site, AIO and snippet pipelines fail: `runtime_compat_subscore = 25`

AEO pillar score:
```
0.40 * 0 + 0.25 * 0 + 0.15 * 70 + 0.10 * 0 + 0.10 * 25
= 0 + 0 + 10.5 + 0 + 2.5
= 13
```

The AEO pillar score is 13 — structurally broken. The runtime sub-score is 25 (reflecting that some runtimes can render JS but most can't). The site is invisible to AEO surfaces despite having strong entity signals off-domain. The clear remediation is server-side rendering of primary content and schema.
